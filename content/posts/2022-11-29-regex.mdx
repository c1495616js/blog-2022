## Quantifiers

- `?`: 0 or 1 time.
- `+`: 1 or more times.
- `*`: 0 or more times.
- `\`: backslash escape
  - ? \* . {} [] () ^ $ (These need to be escaped)
- `{ }`: curly braces
  - `{1,3}`: between one and three times (inclusive)
  - `{3}`: exactly three times
  - `{3,}`: three or more times

### Examples

- `/he+y/`: hey, heey, heeeeeey

## Collections, Ranges, Negation

### Collections

- `[ ]`:
  - one of any of these characters
  - one character after the other with no separation
  - can add quantifier after to indicate how many from collection

### Ranges

- Inside a collection (`[]`)
- Use a hyphen (`-`) to create a range
- ex: `[0123456789ABCDEF]` --> `[0-9A-F]`

### Negation

- `[^<place_holder>]`: anything but `<place_holder>`

## Character Classes

- `\s`: whitespace, `[\r\n\t\v\f]`
- `\S` (capital S): not whitespace, `[^\r\n\t\v\f]`
- `\d`: digit, `[0-9]`
- `\D`: not digit, `[^0-9]`
